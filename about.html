About This Dashboard

India's household economic data is technically public — buried in thousands of PDF tables, district factsheets, and survey microdata annexures produced by NSO, IIPS, and other agencies. This dashboard makes that data explorable.

Each of the 22 indicator pages shows state-level variation through interactive maps, ranked charts, and sortable tables. AI-generated insight blocks surface patterns that would take an analyst hours to find manually.

Who This Is For

Journalists covering the Indian economy, social welfare, or gender equity — for fact-checking, sourcing context, or finding story leads.
Policy researchers who need quick state-to-state comparisons without downloading raw survey files.
Students and educators building literacy in Indian development economics.

Data Vintage

Survey data reflects the most recent published round at time of extraction. Indicators from NFHS-5 (2019–21) pre-date indicators from HCES 2022–23 and PLFS 2022–23. Cross-indicator comparisons should account for this gap. Each page footer shows the exact survey period.

AI Insights Pipeline

Each page's insight blocks are generated by Claude (Anthropic) using the indicator data as the sole context window. The model is instructed to find:

  • Top outlier states (>1.5 standard deviations from the mean)
  • Regional clustering patterns (North / South / East / West / Northeast)
  • Gender dimension findings where splits exist
  • Counter-intuitive findings (wealthy state underperforming, or vice versa)
  • Policy-relevant cross-indicator observations

Every numerical claim in a generated block is automatically verified against the source data before publication. Blocks are assigned one of four verdicts:

supported — exact match mostly_supported — within 2% partly_supported — within 10% — not published unsupported — not published

Only supported and mostly_supported blocks appear on pages. Full factcheck reports (including unpublished blocks) are committed alongside each insights.json as factcheck-report.json.

AI-generated insights are a starting point, not a final word. Verify claims against the primary source before citing in published work. Source URLs are linked from each page's notes section.

Methodology Notes

Economic Cost of IPV (ipv-economic-cost): IPV prevalence figures are observed survey data from NFHS-5. The economic cost estimates are modelled using the WHO productivity-loss methodology (affected women × estimated workdays lost × state daily wage). These are estimates, not official statistics. They are clearly labelled MODELLED on the page.

Ladakh / J&K: Surveys conducted before October 2019 do not distinguish Ladakh as a separate state/UT. For those surveys, Ladakh shows no value on the map.

Missing state data: Grey states on the map indicate that data was not available or published for that state in the source survey round. This is a data gap, not an error.
